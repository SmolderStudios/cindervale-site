❔ How to Play

  • Spin. Each spin costs your bet. Match 3, 4 or 5 of a kind from the left on any payline. The paytable on the left shows every symbol's value and odds — live, as your build changes them. Hover any symbol there for its full detail.
  • The 5 paylines:
    Top row Middle row Bottom row V shape Λ shape
  • Hot streak. Consecutive winning spins boost payouts. A dead spin resets it.
  • Playing faster. AUTO spins for you (10 / 50 / ∞) and pauses itself for the market, a Rite or a duel checkpoint, then carries on once you are done. ⚡ is turbo reels.
  • Keys: Space spin · A autospin · T turbo · 1–0 and - bet · Esc close / stop.
  • Too big or too small? There is a UI size slider on the menu — it scales the whole game, so you can fit it all on one screen or make it readable from the sofa.
  • 👁️ The Eye is wild — it becomes any symbol.
  • 🌕 Moons charge the Blood Moon meter (watch the phase next to your streak). Every Moon on screen pushes it up; at full you get free spins with wins ×2 and your bet locked, then the meter empties and starts again. A Moon is never a dud — it always moves the meter.
  • 🪙 Cursed Gold pays instantly, no line needed. 🕸️ Cobwebs pay nothing — they are the reel's ballast, and they can be thinned but never removed entirely.
  • 🍀 Black Clover multiplies the whole spin. Every Clover on screen adds to the multiplier, so two or three at once turn a modest line into a real win.
  • 🔥 The Hellpot grows with every bet, and its floor scales to the biggest bet you have made — so 6·6·6 on a line is always worth chasing at whatever stakes you play.
  • 🖐️ The Dead Hand. All fifteen cells, one symbol. The rarest thing in the game: it pays a 6× bonus on top of the five lines it already makes AND takes the Hellpot. You cannot force it, but relics and rites make it likelier — and when the reels start landing all-the-same, everything slows down. Two reels matching dims the room. Three drains the colour. Four stops the world.
  • The Reaper's Toll. Every 12 spins Death demands cash. The price climbs geometrically and past the eighth Floor it climbs faster still — the late game is meant to close. It also scales to the stakes you choose, so a $10,000 bettor is asked $10,000-bettor money.
  • Only the Toll can kill you. Going broke mid-round puts you in debt (red cash) — the House lends you spins down to the size of your next Toll. If the Toll arrives and you cannot pay, the run ends.
  • 🌑 The Nights. A run is cut into five Nights. Each recolours the keep, steepens the Toll and makes the Death Roll likelier. Night V is the deep end.
  • ☠️ The Death Roll. A rare bad spin that takes a share of your cash — never the run. It grows more likely the deeper you go, and risk cards raise it further in exchange for power. Warding cards push it back down.
  • Rites. Every Toll you survive, the dark pays you back: pick 1 of 3 run-permanent boons. Many bend the actual spawn odds on the reels — watch the paytable percentages move as you take them. Some starve symbols instead of feeding them, which lifts everything else at once. From Floor 9, Pinnacle Rites appear — they are the answer to the curve.
  • Desperation. The lower your cash falls (and into debt), the luckier the reels get — up to +45% payouts plus more wilds, gold and moons. Losing makes you dangerous.
  • The Night Market. After each Toll, buy card drafts — you are shown several cards and choose which to keep, so you can build toward what you already hold. You may take fewer than the limit, or none at all.
  • Prices climb with every purchase, not with how deep you are — a frugal run keeps cheap options open however far it goes.
  • Relic PERMANENT once played. Duplicates level it up, and relic ladders have no cap — there is always a next level. Charm ONE USE, a temporary boost. Hex ONE USE, strikes your duel opponent.
  • Keystones are the exception: one level only, because each changes a rule rather than a number. Finding one is a reason to steer the whole run around it.
  • Storage. In solo, cards wait in your bag until you choose to play them. Unwanted cards sell for cash. In duels the bag holds Hexes only — relics and charms play themselves the moment you take them, because the only card worth timing is the one you throw.
  • 🩸 Blood is a second currency, and cash cannot buy it. A few symbols are 🔻 marked each run (shown in the paytable). Only a winning line of a marked symbol bleeds Blood.
  • That means Blood rewards committing to a symbol rather than betting big. Rare symbols bleed proportionally more, so no mark set is a dead hand. One mark moves at every Toll, so a build has to keep adapting.
  • The Altar is the only thing Blood buys, and it is the endgame money sink: infinitely repeatable offerings whose price climbs forever. Every symbol family has one, so no single build is the only way to scale. Direct reel weight buys sit underneath as compact chips.
  • The paytable shows your expected Blood per spin and the exact bleed value of every marked symbol — hover them for detail.
  • 🪜 Every Toll is a Floor. The number sits beside the Reaper's Toll.
  • Clearing Floor 20 is an escape — the only way a run ends well. It pays a Souls bonus and a ◈ Sigil, the one currency Souls cannot buy.
  • The Black Grimoire is bought with Sigils. Unlike the Souls Grimoire it does not pad your numbers — it bends the run: an extra Rite choice, wider drafts, a relic already in play, free rerolls, starting Blood, skipping the first floor.
  • Descents. Escaping opens the next one: the same keep with heavier Tolls, paying more Sigils. Eight of them. That is the long game.
  • Trials are goals that persist across every run and pay Souls once — check them from the menu for something to chase once the basics stop scaring you.
  • Death is progress. You keep Souls for permanent Grimoire pacts, and every card you pull joins your Collection forever.
  • ⚔️ Duels. Invite a friend by lobby code. You each fight your own reels — last one alive wins. Hexes sabotage them; their panel shows every card they play and mirrors their reels live.
  • Tolls are checkpoints. After your Toll you shop, then press ✔ Ready — neither player spins the next round until both are ready, so nobody races ahead while you browse.
  • Underdog & Grudge. Falling behind grants Underdog (up to +30% payouts and more wilds). Every hex you eat and every Toll you spend behind fills the Grudge meter — at 100 it forces a free Hex into your hand. The leader never gets to coast.
  • Mercy exists. Your opponent's panel has 💸 Send, and you have 🙏 Beg — a rich rival can bail you out of debt to keep the duel going.
  • 🗡️ Co-op: The Hunt. Same lobby, pick The Hunt instead. You each spin your own reels and pay your own Tolls, but Scythes landing on either player's reels fill a shared Summoning meter. At full, a boss rises for both of you.
  • Wounding a boss. Every winning line hurts it, and the wound is that line's payout multiple × your multiplier for that symbol × 30 — so it comes from your BUILD, not your bet. While a boss is up the paytable shows a 🗡️ column with exactly what each symbol wounds for. Pairs and Cursed Gold pay cash but deal nothing.
  • 🎴 A Hunt has no bag. A card you take is played immediately. When you pick one you can click it again to send it to your partner instead, and it plays on their side.
  • Shared fate. If either of you falls, the hunt ends — so keep your partner solvent, and cover their Toll when the button appears.
